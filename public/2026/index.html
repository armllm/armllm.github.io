Program overview

When and where is the summer school happening?

August 3–7, 2026 at the AI9 Startup Campus (9 Isahakyan St, Yerevan, Armenia).

Is there an online option?

Yes. We offer an online track via Zoom covering the theoretical sessions. Online participants typically do not get access to shared GPU resources.

Will there be a hackathon?

Yes. The hackathon starts on August 8 at 00:00 and runs for a full 24-hour hack day. It is co-organized with our partner AI9.

Eligibility & background

Who should apply?

The program is designed for motivated learners in AI, including students, researchers, and engineers.

Do I need prior LLM experience?

No. Prior hands-on LLM experience is not required, but a foundational understanding of machine learning and neural networks is expected.

Do I need Python experience?

Python proficiency is expected and is asked in the application form.

Application & selection

How are applicants selected?

Selection is competitive and follows a two-stage process: (1) initial application form and (2) technical assessment.

What do I need for registration?

The form asks for personal/contact details, affiliation, experience, Python proficiency, and a short motivation paragraph. You must upload your CV/resume.

What is the application timeline for 2026?
  • Stage 1: submit the form and CV by May 15, 2026.
  • Stage 2: monitor email for a test link on May 28, 2026.
  • Answers: expect to receive an answer by June 5.

If you do not receive the technical test link, contact armeniallm@gmail.com.

Fees & financial aid

How much does the program cost?
  • In-person: 100,000 AMD (or equivalent). Includes GPU access.
  • Online: $200 (or equivalent). Theoretical sessions via Zoom.
Is there financial aid?

Yes. Students can apply for waivers that can cover up to 90% of the fee. Depending on available funds, we may offer discounts to other participants as well.

Can my company pay for me?

Yes. We can issue a prepayment invoice for your employer and provide an official tax invoice after the program ends if needed.

Logistics & policies

What should I bring?

A laptop is required for hands-on and practical sessions.

Do you provide housing or food?

We do not provide accommodation directly, but we usually recommend the Yerevan State University guesthouse (price example from 2025). We provide coffee and snacks during breaks; lunch and dinner are on your own.

How do we stay updated during the school?

Once you are confirmed, you will get an invite to our Slack channel for agenda updates and session links.

Will I get a certificate? What about refunds?

Everyone who fully attends receives a Certificate of Participation. If you let us know early enough, we can usually issue a refund so we can offer your spot to someone on the waitlist.
