Armenia LLM Summer School (August 3-7, 2026, Yerevan) connects local students and engineers with frontier AI research through intensive lectures and practical sessions.

Why we are fundraising

  • Invite up to 5 international researchers to teach in person.
  • Cover flights and accommodation for invited researchers.
  • Support Armenian university students with discounts up to 90%.

What registration fees cover

  • Non-student participants usually pay around USD 300.
  • Those registrations are used to run core school operations.
  • Donor contributions are key for researcher travel + student aid.

Funding priority 1
International researchers

Invite and host up to 5 international researchers

Donor funding is primarily needed for travel and accommodation so top researchers can teach in person and mentor participants.

What this funding covers

  • Round-trip flights for invited researchers.
  • Accommodation during the program period.
  • Core hosting costs needed for teaching participation.

Expected donor impact

Every supported researcher directly increases lecture quality, mentorship depth, and the international visibility of Armenia's AI ecosystem.

Why donor support is critical here

Registration income from non-student participants (about USD 300 each) is allocated to school organization costs. External funding allows us to keep this researcher-in-residence model viable each year.

Funding priority 2
Student access

Support Armenian university students with up to 90% discounts

Financial aid keeps participation merit-based and accessible for high-potential students who could not otherwise afford the program.

Student aid model

  • Target discount level: up to 90% for Armenian university students.
  • Aid decisions are based on merit and financial need.
  • Goal: keep the strongest candidates in the room regardless of income.

Why this matters

  • Removes a major barrier for local talent entering advanced AI pathways.
  • Improves diversity across universities and backgrounds.
  • Creates long-term national capacity in LLM research and engineering.

Simple funding logic

Tuition from non-student registrations helps operate the school; donor gifts close the gap that makes deep student discounts possible.
